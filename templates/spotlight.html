Calamari

Course: Appetizer
Cuisine: Filipino
Servings: 4
Prep Time: 10 mins
Cooking Time: 10 mins
Total Time: 20 mins
Ingredients: 0.5 kg calamari rings soaked in milk for 3 hours, 1/2 cup all-purpose flour, 1 1/2 teaspoon salt, 2 teaspoons garlic powder, 2 teaspoons dried parsley flakes, Lemon wedges and 2 cups cooking oil
Step 1: Combine the flour, salt, garlic powder, and parsley flakes. Mix well and set aside. Heat the cooking oil in a wok or a deep cooking pot.
Step 2: Drain the milk of the calamari. Dredge the calamari rings one-at-a-time on the flour mixture. Make sure that the calamari ring is completely coated with the mixture.
Step 3: Deep fry the coated calamari rings on hot oil for 2 minutes or until the calamari turns light brown.
Step 4: Strain the calamari from the wok or cooking pot using a skimmer or strainer. Let the excess oil drip.
Step 5: Transfer the fried calamari in a serving plate. Serve with lemon wedges.

Quick Sushi Bowl

Course: Street Food
Cuisine: Japanese
Servings: 2
Prep Time: 15 mins
Cooking Time: 20 mins
Total Time: 35 mins
Ingredients: 150g sushi rice, pinch sugar, 1 tbsp rice vinegar, 2 cooked salmon fillets (use teriyaki marinated), 1 large carrot cut into ribbons, 4 tbsp pickled red cabbage, handful radishes sliced, thumb-sized piece ginger finely sliced, ¼ cucumber , halved longways and sliced, 1 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tsp sesame seeds
Step 1: Cook the sushi rice following pack instructions.
Step 2: When cooked, sprinkle over the sugar and vinegar, cover and set aside for 5 mins.
Step 3: Divide the rice between two bowls.
Step 4: Arrange the other ingredients on top.
Step 5: Then drizzle with the soy sauce and sesame seeds.

Pad Thai with Tofu

Course: Main Course
Cuisine: Thai
Servings: 4
Prep Time: 30 mins
Cooking Time: 30 mins
Total Time: 1 hr
Ingredients: 80g roughly chopped roasted peanuts, 2 tsp chilli flakes, 3 peeled garlic cloves, 2 tbsp light muscovado sugar, 2 tbsp vegetable oil, 280g cubed extra-firm tofu, 360g peeled, sustainable raw king prawns, 200g thinly sliced, 500g chard, shallots, Chilli peanut paste, 150g cooked flat rice noodles, 2 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tbsp fish sauce, Juice of 2 limes and Extra peanuts to serve
Step 1: Grind the roasted peanuts to a rough paste in a pestle and mortar with the chilli flakes, garlic cloves, muscovado sugar and a small handful of chopped coriander stalks.
Step 2: Heat the oil in a large frying pan over a medium heat and fry the cubed extra-firm tofu until golden, then scoop out and drain on kitchen paper. Add the king prawns to the pan. Fry until just pink, then remove and set aside.
Step 3: Reduce the heat and fry the shallots until golden, then add the chilli peanut paste and fry for 2-3 minutes. Finely chop the chard, keeping the stalks and leaves separate.
Step 4: Add the stalks to the pan and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until beginning to soften. Add the flat rice noodles to the pan, then return the tofu and prawns to the pan with the chard leaves.
Step 5: Stir-fry to mix and heat through. Season with soy sauce, fish sauce and the lime juice. Serve with extra peanuts sprinkled on top and lime wedges.

Youtiao (Doughnuts)

Course: Dessert
Cuisine: Chinese
Servings: 5
Prep Time: 1 hr
Cooking Time: 30 mins
Total Time: 1 hr 30 mins
Ingredients: 200 g all-purpose flour (ordinary gluten flour), 110 ml cold water, 3 tablespoons peanut oil (about 45 ml), pinch of salt and 2 ½ teaspoons baking powder
Step 1: Sift the flour, baking powder and salt into a bowl. Add the peanut oil. Pour in the cold water and mix well with chopsticks. Mix the dough until it is smooth and elastic. This takes about 3 minutes. Put the dough back into the bowl, cover closely with plastic wrap and put aside to rest for an hour.
Step 2: Take the dough from the bowl and flatten it with your hands to a thickness of about ½ inch. Fold in half. Press the dough down with your fist. Turn the dough 45 degrees, fold in half again and press down. Continue turning (always in the same direction), folding and pressing ten times. Flip the dough over and repeat ten more times. Continue this process of kneading for a good 5 minutes. All this stretching and stressing strengthens the dough, so that after about 5 minutes it will become difficult to knead signalling the time to stop. Place the dough in a plastic bag or wrap in plastic wrap, smoothing out any air pockets. Put it into the refrigerator (at +4 degrees Celsius) overnight.
Step 3: The next day, remove the dough from the refrigerator and let stand for 45 minutes at room temperature. Brush a layer of peanut oil on a clean chopping board to prevent the dough sticking. Roll the dough into a log about 30 cm long.
Step 4: Flatten the dough to a width of about 8-10 cm, cover with plastic wrap and leave while you prepare the pan for cooking. To save on oil, the pan can be small, but not too small, or the doughnuts will be too short. A pan with a diameter of 24 cm is ideal but needs to be deep enough to fill it with about 3-4 cm of oil. For safety reasons, it is best to use a double handled pot, to reduce the chance of knocking it and spilling the hot oil, the consequences of which are too scary to contemplate. It is also good to keep the oil level at a distance of at least 3cm from the top edge of the pan to reduce spillage. After pouring oil into your pan, turn the hotplate too high.
Step 5: The most simple and reliable way to test the temperature is to throw in a small piece of dough, and if it starts to bubble and begins to expand within 5 seconds, the oil is ready. Cut two strips about 1.5-2 cm wide and lay one on top of the other. Press them together lengthwise with bamboo sticks or fine chopsticks. Gently pinch the ends and slowly stretch until the piece of dough is a bit shorter than the width of the pan. Place the doughnut gently into the hot oil and quickly roll with chopsticks so it expands evenly. Fry quickly until it has expanded and floats to the top. When the doughnut is nice and golden and is nicely inflated, remove it from the oil and drain on paper towels.
